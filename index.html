What is Monte Carlo Simulation?

Monte Carlo simulation uses random sampling to model the range of possible outcomes for your trading strategy. Instead of just calculating a single theoretical return, it runs many different trade sequences using your win rate, R:R and risk %, then shows the resulting equity curves.

This tool runs many simulated trading paths using your parameters, then highlights the best, most probable and worst outcomes. This gives you a realistic view of your strategy’s risk and reward profile – not just the average.

How to Use This Tool
  • Step 1: Enter your verified win rate and R:R from backtests or live stats.
  • Step 2: Set your typical risk per trade and number of trades you want to simulate.
  • Step 3: Enter your starting capital (e.g. prop account size or personal account).
  • Step 4: Run the simulation and review Best, Most Probable and Worst tabs.
  • Step 5: Use the Probability Analysis to understand the likelihood of different outcomes.
Understanding Key Metrics
  • Return (Compounded): Total percentage gain/loss with position sizing that grows as your account grows.
  • Return (R): Total R-multiples gained relative to initial risk size. Shows pure strategy performance.
  • Expectancy: Expected return per trade in R-multiples. Positive expectancy = strategy edge.
  • Max Drawdown: Worst peak-to-trough decline experienced in that equity path.
  • Max Consecutive Wins / Losses: Longest streaks – helps you prepare psychologically for hot and cold runs.
Common Interpretation Pitfalls
  • Overconfidence in Best Case: High-flying outcomes are possible but not guaranteed. Don’t plan your rent around them.
  • Ignoring Drawdown: Even profitable strategies can have sharp equity dips. Make sure you can sit through them.
  • Static Parameters: Real trading performance fluctuates; this model assumes fixed win rate and R:R.
  • Position Sizing Behaviour: Simulation assumes you consistently risk the same % – no revenge trading or flinching.
  • Sequence Risk: The same average stats can feel very different depending on where losing streaks land.
How Metrics Are Calculated
  • Expectancy Formula: (Win Rate × Average Win R) − (Loss Rate × Average Loss R).
  • Max Drawdown: Maximum percentage decline from any equity peak to a later trough.
  • Return (R): (Final Balance − Initial Capital) ÷ (Initial Capital × Risk % per trade).
  • Compounded Return: (Final Balance ÷ Initial Capital − 1) × 100.
  • Probability Ranges: Based on final % returns across all simulated paths, split into Loss, Small, Medium and Large gain buckets.
